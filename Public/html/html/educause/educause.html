The Sakai Project

The Sakai Project is a community source effort to design, build and deploy a new Collaboration and Learning Environment for higher education. The Sakai Project’s primary goal is to deliver the Sakai application framework and tools for course management and research collaboration. The Sakai software is in use for learning and research support at many institutions, and as open source, is freely available for download. Founded in January 2004 by the University of Michigan, Indiana University, Stanford, MIT, uPortal and the Open Knowledge Initiative, and supported in part by a grant from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation, the Sakai project has grown to over 80 institutions around the world through the Sakai Educational partners Program. Sakai provides a support mechanism for institutions that want to be involved in the Sakai project. The next meeting of the Sakai consortium is December 7-9, in Austin, Texas.

Kuali Project

The Kuali Project is developing a modular suite of open source financial software to meet the needs of all Carnegie Class institutions. The Kuali Financial System (KFS) includes a Flexible Chart of Accounts, General Ledger, A/R, Capital Asset Management, Purchasing, A/P, Cash Receipting and Disbursement, Travel Requisition and Reimbursement, Auxiliary Accounting, and Budget Construction. Kuali Research Administration (KRA) includes support for Pre and Post-Award Administration. Both KFS and KRA make use of XML-based OneStart© workflow for routing and approval of transactions. The Kuali Project Founders include Indiana, Arizona, Cornell, Hawaii, Michigan State, San Joaquin Delta, NACUBO, and rSmart. Kuali is funded in part by a grant from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation.

kualiproject.org

 

Open Source Portfolio

The Open Source Portfolio (OSP) brings together a community of individuals and organizations collaborating on the development of the leading non-proprietary, open source electronic portfolio software available. The Open Source Portfolio Release 2 is suited for active reflection, assessment, career development, and institutional review. It can be used stand alone or integrated with any Learning Management System. The Open Source Portfolio software is being developed at Indiana University, The rSmart Group, DePaul, Virginia Tech, Minnesota, and with help from a worldwide community including Syracuse and University of Amsterdam. It is funded in part by a grant from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation. All OSP software is free to download, use, and extend, and the OSP community invites collaboration to further the effective use of portfolios. OSP will hold its next conference on December 7-9, 2005 in Austin, Texas.

osportfolio.org
