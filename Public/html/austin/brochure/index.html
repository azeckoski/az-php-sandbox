The 4th Sakai Conference, in Austin, Texas, December 7 to 9, is a time for celebrating and working. Come join us in celebrating the achievements of the Sakai Community over the past two years, and work with us to create the innovative, open, community source future.  We are becoming a rich and energized community of individual, institutional, and commercial members. This fall has seen an increasing number of campus production and pilot implementations of Sakai and a number of new contributions, tools and partnerships. 

In one of these new partnerships, the OSP community is joining the Sakai community at this conference, strengthening both of our efforts to bring innovative tools and methods to education, research and the global learning community.

Come to Austin ready to share, learn, make connections and collaborate, and of course to have some fun!

Everyone Welcome

The Sakai conference program is being designed to strengthen the growing Sakai community by bringing the entire community together to share their Sakai vision, experiences, and implementations. It is also designed to welcome people interested in learning about community source software and seeing firsthand how the Sakai community operates. Accordingly, there are sessions that will be devoted to the future of the codebase, Sakai governance and the new Sakai Foundation, and the integration of other community source projects such as OSP, as well as sessions introducing newbies to the software and the community. There will also be new tracks that focus on teaching and learning, on eResearch, and on other related projects, such as OpenCourseWare effort and Sakai, OKI, IMS, and much more.  And you can create a BOF (Birds of a Feather meeting) on the topic of most importance to you, or a topic you wan to learn more about.

Sakai conferences have become major twice yearly events with attendance increasing significantly with each conference. We therefore expect a very large gathering in Austin and we have planned facilities accordingly.  Register early to guarantee a place.

OSP is the Open Source Portfolio, which runs within Sakai technology. OSPI has met separately at conferences in the summer of 2004 and 2005 but for this and future conferences will combine communities with Sakai. The Open Source Portfolio Initiative also has Mellon funding, and a number of universities and the rSmart group supporting it.

For those concerned, we have been in communication with our hotel in Austin regarding the impact of New Orleans conference rescheduling on available space in Austin and have been reassured we are still fully on track for December 7-9 in Austin. Y'all come!
Conference Tracks
Teaching, Learning, and Assessment
The Teaching, Learning, and Assessment track will offer opportunuties to share experiences with and plans for using Sakai or OSP for teaching and learning. These sessions will include presentations on the impact of OSP (or portfolios in general) on teaching practices; discussions regarding the value of a Collaborative Learning Environment (CLE) and its integrated toolset for students and the learning process; assessment management systems for tracking student progress toward learning goals; online learning using Sakai/OSP; and supporting reflective practices.
Management & Campus Implementation
This track highlights issues of concern to those managing current or upcoming pilot implementations; campus impact and implications; project assessment implications; training; coordination and leadership; and institutional change. This is appropriate for implementations of Sakai, OSP, or any interest regarding other strategies for integration of Sakai tools.
Research and Collaboration
The Research and Collaboration track will focus on using the Sakai Collaboration and Learning Environment (CLE) and its tools such as OSP or Melete, can be used to support  research collaborations. We are looking for existing examples, current or upcoming projects, uses cases, and plans.
Technology and User Interface
The Technology and User Interface track will offer sessions, panels, and round-tables that cover features and requirements; technical challenges and lessons learned for installing and implementing Sakai and integrated tools such as OSP, Samigo, and Melete; contributing code; tech support; commercial tech support; future releases; usability, user-interface design, and user-testing.
Sakai Foundation, Community Source & Governance
These sessions will focus on the transition from the current organization to the Sakai Foundation as the operational, governance, and legal framework for Sakai and OSPI. Issues and process around open and community source licensing and copyright will also be covered here.
